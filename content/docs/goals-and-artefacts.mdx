---
title: Goals & Artefacts
description: Workspace goals, intent artefacts, snapshots, and reconciliation outcomes — what they are, how Vaner uses them, and where they show up.
---

Vaner persists two long-horizon concepts that span beyond a single conversation
or prepared-work card: **workspace goals** and **intent artefacts**. Together
they let Vaner stay focused on what you actually want to accomplish, even
across days of work and across different AI clients.

## Workspace goals

A **goal** is a long-horizon intent in your workspace — "ship the new auth
middleware", "remove the legacy token storage path", "answer pending design
questions in the streaming pipeline". Goals are persisted to
`.vaner/artefacts.db` so they survive daemon restarts, and they outlive any
individual prompt.

Each goal carries:

- a **status** (`active`, `paused`, `abandoned`, `achieved`)
- **related files** — the paths Vaner has seen the goal touched on
- a **reconciliation state** (`reconciled` / `unreconciled` / partial) — Vaner's
  best-effort assessment of whether the goal's evidence is consistent with
  what's currently in the repo

Goals can be declared explicitly or inferred from the work you do. The
[`vaner.goals`](/integrations/mcp) MCP family lets agents create, list, update, and delete
them; the [cockpit](/cockpit) shows them with linked artefacts and the latest
reconciliation outcome.

## Intent artefacts

An **artefact** is a piece of intent-bearing content tied to a source — a
design doc, an issue, a Slack thread, a code comment, a README section. Vaner
ingests these through connectors, snapshots them periodically, and uses the
snapshots to detect drift between the artefact and the current state of the
repo.

Each artefact has:

- a **source URI** and **connector** (where it came from)
- a **status** (`active`, `dormant`, `stale`, `superseded`, `archived`)
- a **latest snapshot** — versioned content that lets Vaner reason about change
- **linked goals** — which long-horizon intents this artefact supports

Artefact items inside a snapshot are the granular pieces — a section, a bullet
list, a paragraph — that Vaner extracts so individual claims can be tracked
across snapshots.

## Reconciliation outcomes

When the repo changes (a commit lands, a file changes, a dependency updates),
Vaner runs reconciliation: for each artefact, does its current snapshot still
agree with the code? The result is a **reconciliation outcome** that records:

- which **signal** triggered the pass (commit hash, file change, category shift)
- per-item **state deltas** — what's now stale, what's confirmed, what's been
  superseded
- per-goal **status deltas** — does this signal move any goal toward
  `achieved` or away from it?
- **supersedes** — when a newer artefact replaces an older one

Reconciliation outcomes feed back into the prepared-work pipeline. A goal whose
linked artefacts have all gone stale gets demoted; an artefact that gains
confirming evidence in a new commit gets promoted.

## Where you'll see them

- **MCP** — `vaner.goals.list`, `vaner.goals.declare`, `vaner.goals.update_status`,
  `vaner.goals.delete` for goal CRUD; `vaner.artefacts.list`,
  `vaner.artefacts.inspect`, `vaner.artefacts.set_status`,
  `vaner.artefacts.influence` for artefact navigation. See [MCP](/integrations/mcp).
- **Cockpit** — the `Goals` destination in the top bar shows declared goals
  inline with their linked artefacts and the most recent reconciliation
  outcome. See [Cockpit](/cockpit).
- **CLI** — `vaner inspect --last` and `vaner doctor` surface reconciliation
  state when investigating why a particular Prepared Work item was prepared
  the way it was.

## Why this matters

Without goals + artefacts, Vaner would be a context-fetcher with amnesia —
useful for a single prompt, useless across a week of related work. The
goals/artefacts layer is what lets Vaner stay aligned with what you're trying
to accomplish over the long arc, and what lets it admit when its assumptions
are stale instead of confidently serving outdated evidence.

The non-mutation guarantee still holds: Vaner can mark a goal achieved or
demote a stale artefact, but it never edits your code or your source documents
to make them match its model.
